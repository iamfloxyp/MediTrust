Drug Description

  • Tramadol’s mechanism of action involves two components: opioid agonism and inhibition of serotonin and norepinephrine reuptake.Its opioid activity primarily involves binding to mu-opioid receptors, resulting in analgesia and reduced perception of pain.Tramadol also inhibits the reuptake of serotonin and norepinephrine, enhancing their activity in the central nervous system and contributing to its analgesic effects.

  • Tramadol is an opioid analgesic used to manage moderate to moderately severe pain.It works by binding to opioid receptors in the brain, altering the perception of pain.Tramadol is available in various forms including immediate-release tablets, extended-release tablets, and capsules.It’s typically prescribed in doses ranging from 50 to 100 mg every 4 to 6 hours as needed for pain relief.
